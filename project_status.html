⚠️ Open Challenges in Making AI Traceable
Real product gaps that prevent this from being production-grade interpretability tooling
🔬
Head ↔ Behavior Causality Not Proven
We can ablate heads and observe output changes, but proving those heads *cause* hallucinations vs correlate with them is unsolved. Need counterfactual validation and ground-truth benchmarks.
HIGH RISK
🎯
No Ground-Truth Validation
Without a dataset of known hallucination circuits, we can't measure false positives. Ranking heuristics are heuristic — we don't know if we're finding real causal circuits or just noise.
HIGH RISK
📊
Scalability Gap: Head Explosion
GPT-4 class models have 100B+ parameters. Pair ablation of all heads is O(n²). Current approach doesn't scale. Need intelligent pruning or hierarchical discovery before full sweeps.
HIGH RISK
🔗
Token-Level vs Layer-Level Tracing
Current traces operate at layer level. But hallucinations often emerge from specific token-to-token interactions. Need finer-grained token-level attribution.
MEDIUM
🧠
No Contextual Understanding
Same head may be defective for some prompts but critical for others. Current masking is binary. Need prompt-type aware masking or dynamic quarantine.
MEDIUM
⏱️
Latency Overhead
PyTorch hooks add inference latency. For production, tracing must be near-zero-cost. Current implementation is research-grade, not production-grade.
MEDIUM
🔭 The Path Forward
To make AI truly traceable, we need: (1) ground-truth hallucination datasets to validate circuits, (2) scalable discovery algorithms that don't require O(n²) ablation, (3) token-level attribution instead of layer-level, (4) production-grade tracing with minimal latency overhead. This project is the foundation — these are the research problems that remain open.
